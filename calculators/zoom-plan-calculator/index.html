üí° How to Choose the Right Zoom Plan

The 40-Minute Rule: Basic (free) plan limits group meetings to 40 minutes. If you regularly run team meetings longer than 40 minutes, Pro ($15.99/month) pays for itself by eliminating the need to restart meetings.

Participant Math: Pro supports 100 participants, Business supports 300, Enterprise supports 1,000. If you need 101-300, you MUST get Business plan ($21.99/host) which requires minimum 10 hosts ($219.90/month minimum). Consider alternatives like Google Meet for this range.

Cloud Recording Economics: Pro includes 5GB cloud storage ($80/year value). Business includes unlimited cloud storage (worth $200+/year for active teams). If you record meetings frequently, Business plan's storage alone justifies the upgrade.

Per-Host Pricing Trap: Zoom charges per host (someone who can start meetings), not per participant. If only 3 people need to host meetings, buy 3 licenses even if 50 people attend. Participants join free.

Webinar Add-On Costs: Webinars (broadcast-style with Q&A) are separate from meetings. Costs $40-790/month extra depending on attendee count. For large audiences, consider YouTube Live (free) or Crowdcast ($29/month) instead.

Annual Billing Saves 17%: Zoom offers ~17% discount on annual billing. Pro drops to $13.32/month (save $31.80/year), Business to $18.32/month (save $43.20/year per host). Lock in if you're committed.

Free Alternatives for Small Teams: Google Meet (free, unlimited duration with Workspace), Microsoft Teams (free, 60-min limit), or Whereby ($9.99/month, unlimited, up to 100 people) often beat Zoom for small teams.
