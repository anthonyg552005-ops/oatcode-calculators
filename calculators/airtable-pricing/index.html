💡 Tips for Choosing Your Airtable Plan

Start with Free: If you're just getting started with Airtable, begin with the free plan to test if it fits your workflow. The 1,200 records per base is enough for many small projects.

Watch Your Record Count: The biggest limitation across plans is the number of records per base. If you're managing large datasets (customer lists, inventory, etc.), you'll need Plus (5,000) or Pro (50,000) tier.

Automation is Key: Free plans get 100 automation runs/month, Plus gets 5,000, and Pro gets 25,000. If you're automating repetitive tasks, this quickly becomes a bottleneck on lower tiers.

Consider Per-Seat Pricing: Airtable charges per user, so a 10-person team on Pro ($20/seat) costs $200/month. Consider if all team members need edit access or if some can be comment-only collaborators (free).

Annual Billing Saves 20%: All paid Airtable plans offer ~20% discount when billed annually instead of monthly. Worth it if you're committed long-term.
