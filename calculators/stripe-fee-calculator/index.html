üìä How to Minimize Payment Processing Fees

Pass Fees to Customers (Legally): Many states allow you to add a "service fee" to cover card processing. A $100 product becomes $103 with 3% fee passed through. This is legal in 48 states (CO and CT prohibit it). Stripe Checkout supports this natively.

Use ACH for Large Transactions: For payments over $625, ACH (0.8% capped at $5) beats cards (2.9% + 30¬¢). A $1,000 ACH payment costs $5 vs $29.30 for a card. Offer ACH option for invoices over $500.

Optimize for Domestic Cards: International cards cost 1% more (3.9% vs 2.9%). If possible, use a local Stripe account for each region. Stripe supports 45+ countries with local processing.

Bundle Transactions: Instead of charging $10 four times ($1.20 in fees at 2.9% + 30¬¢ each), charge $40 once ($1.46 in fees). The fixed 30¬¢ kills small transactions. Minimum order values reduce fee burden.

Negotiate Volume Pricing: At $1M+/year volume, contact Stripe for custom pricing. You can negotiate down to 2.2-2.5% + 30¬¢. At $10M+/year, you can get interchange-plus pricing (1.5-1.8% + interchange).

Use Stripe Billing for Subscriptions: Subscriptions have the same 2.9% + 30¬¢ fee, but Stripe Billing automates dunning (retry failed payments), reducing churn by 30-40%. The recovered revenue offsets fees.

Avoid Currency Conversion Fees: If selling internationally, collect in local currency (EUR, GBP, JPY). Customers prefer it, and you avoid the 1% conversion fee. Stripe supports 135+ currencies.
